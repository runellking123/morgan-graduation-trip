Biography

A life in education

Dr. Morgan Bennett King is the Assistant Provost at Wiley University in Marshall, Texas, where she provides executive oversight of academic departments and faculty affairs. A native of Baytown, Texas, she is the daughter of Marvin and Lilly Bennett and a graduate of Ross S. Sterling High School.

Dr. King earned a Bachelor of Science in Health Science from Lamar University, followed by a Master of Education and an Educational Specialist degree in Curriculum and Instruction from Grand Canyon University. In June 2026, she will receive her Doctor of Education (Ed.D.) from Capella University, completing a doctoral journey that demanded persistence through significant personal and professional adversity.

Her career in education began in elementary and middle school classrooms across Houston, Texas; New Orleans and Baton Rouge, Louisiana; and White Castle, Louisiana, where she built lasting relationships with students and families in each community she served. She transitioned into secondary education administration in Baton Rouge before advancing into higher education leadership as Assistant Dean of Education at St. Augustine University in Raleigh, North Carolina. Her trajectory reflects a consistent commitment to academic quality, institutional effectiveness, and the success of every student she touches.

Dr. King is a proud member of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated. She is married to Dr. Runell J. King, and together they are raising two sons, Kai (age 5) and Khol (age 2). Outside of work, she enjoys cooking, traveling — she will follow anywhere her iPhone maps can lead — and staying close to family through daily FaceTime calls with her mother and her brother-in-law, Dr. Jovan T. Thomas.
