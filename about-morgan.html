About Dr. Morgan King

Educator, scholar, leader, daughter, wife, mother, and proud member of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated.

Hometown & roots

Morgan Bennett King grew up in Baytown, Texas, where she was raised with love, structure, and high expectations. She is the daughter of Marvin and Lilly Bennett. She graduated from Ross S. Sterling High School, carrying forward the discipline and curiosity that would shape her career.

Family

Morgan shares her life with her husband, Dr. Runell J. King. Together they are raising two sons: Kai King (age 5) and Khol King (age 2). Family — near and extended — remains at the center of how she moves through the world.

Education

Morgan’s academic path reflects a deep commitment to health, teaching, and leadership:

  • Bachelor of Science, Health Science — Lamar University
  • Master of Education (M.Ed.), Curriculum & Instruction — Grand Canyon University
  • Educational Specialist (Ed.S.), Curriculum & Instruction — Grand Canyon University
  • Doctor of Education (Ed.D.) — Capella University

Career

Morgan began her professional life in the classroom. She served as an elementary and middle school teacher in Houston, Texas; New Orleans, Louisiana; Baton Rouge, Louisiana; and White Castle, Louisiana — building relationships with students and families across communities.

She moved into secondary education administration in Baton Rouge, then advanced into higher education. She previously served as an Assistant Dean of Education at St. Augustine University in Raleigh, North Carolina, and has held roles as Assistant Dean and professor in education.

Today, Morgan serves as Assistant Provost at Wiley University in Marshall, Texas, where she oversees academic departments and faculty. Her work sits at the intersection of academic quality, student success, and institutional leadership.

Professional & community life

Morgan is a member of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated, carrying forward a tradition of service and scholarship.

Beyond work

She enjoys cooking, travel (she will happily follow anywhere her iPhone maps can lead), and FaceTiming with her mother and her brother-in-law, Dr. Jovan T. Thomas. Completing her doctorate required persistence through real adversity; she stayed the course, finished the work, and modeled for her sons what steady commitment looks like.
