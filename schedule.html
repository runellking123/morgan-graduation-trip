What to Wear

Graduate (Morgan): Full doctoral regalia (cap, gown, hood) provided or purchased through Capella. Wear comfortable dress shoes underneath — you'll be standing and walking. Professional attire beneath the gown.

Guests: Semi-formal / dressy casual. Think church-appropriate attire. Suggested colors: navy, gold, white to match the celebration theme. Comfortable shoes for walking. South Florida in June is hot and humid — lightweight fabrics recommended.

Kids: Clean, neat outfits. Bring a change of clothes for beach time after.

Parking Tips

• The Broward County Convention Center has an on-site parking garage. Arrive by 8:45 AM for the best spots near the entrance.

• Parking usually runs about $10–$20. Our van is tall — if a garage feels tight, use an outdoor lot or street parking nearby.

• Alternative: park on nearby side streets (Eisenhower Blvd area) — often free on Saturdays.

• Consider dropping Morgan off at the graduate entrance before parking the van.

Guest Ticket Info

• Morgan picks up guest tickets at the graduate check-in desk upon arrival (before 9:00 AM line-up).

• 6 tickets guaranteed. Since your group has 8 people total (including Morgan), that means 7 guests need entry. Coordinate with Capella's graduation team in advance to request additional tickets or confirm overflow viewing options.

• Tip: Contact Capella's Commencement Office at least 2 weeks before to inquire about extra guest tickets or livestream options for any overflow guests.

Photo Op Suggestions Near the Convention Center

• Convention Center Main Entrance: Grand signage and columns make a classic backdrop.

• Intracoastal Waterway (steps away): Beautiful water views with yachts and the city skyline. Walk east toward the marina.

• Port Everglades Inlet: 5-minute walk south — dramatic ocean views where cruise ships pass.

• Fort Lauderdale Beach (A1A): 5-minute drive east. Palm trees, ocean, and sand for celebratory photos.

• Riverwalk Fort Lauderdale: 10-minute drive north. Scenic park along the New River with art installations and lush landscaping.

• Tip: Bring a small handheld sign or banner that reads “Dr. Morgan King” for photos. Gold and navy balloons photograph beautifully against the waterfront.

Day 1

Thursday, June 25 — Road Trip: Houston to Fort Lauderdale (Straight Through)
4:00 AM
Wake up & cook breakfast at home. Eat a full meal before hitting the road. Final packing, load cooler with snacks and drinks, kid entertainment ready.
5:00 AM
Depart Houston. Runell drives first. Head east on I-10. Everyone else sleeps or settles in. ~1,100 miles ahead.

12:00 PM
Lunch stop in New Orleans/Metairie area. Quick sit-down or drive-through: Popeyes, Raising Cane's, or a Cajun spot off I-10. ~30 min. Fill up gas.
